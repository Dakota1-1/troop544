What We Do

Our program runs throughout the year with regular meetings, monthly outdoor adventures, and special events that bring scouts together.

Weekly · Year-Round
Troop Meetings
Every Wednesday evening from 7:30 to 9:00 PM at Manor Oaks School. Meetings include skill instruction, patrol activities, rank advancement work, and planning for upcoming events. All scouts — and prospective new members — are welcome. First visits are always free with no commitment required.
Every Wednesday 7:30–9:00 PM Manor Oaks School All ranks welcome
Monthly · Year-Round
Camping Trips
Troop 544 goes camping approximately once a month throughout the year. Camping trips are planned by the scouts themselves, supervised by trained adult leaders. Destinations vary — from local parks and council camps to more ambitious multi-day wilderness outings. Camping trips are where scouts earn key outdoor skills badges, practice cooking, navigation, and first aid, and build the bonds that define the scouting experience.
Monthly Various locations Overnight trips Skills-focused
Annual · Summer
Summer Camp
Each summer, Troop 544 attends a week-long BSA council camp where scouts can earn multiple merit badges, participate in aquatics, shooting sports, STEM programs, and high-adventure activities under expert instruction. Summer camp is one of the most memorable experiences in a Scout's career — a full week away from home building independence, friendships, and skills.
Annual — Summer One week BSA Council Camp Multiple merit badges
Annual · Spring/Fall
District Camporee
The Theodore Roosevelt Council hosts annual Camporees — multi-troop events where scouts from across Nassau County come together to compete in scouting skills, participate in themed activities, and foster inter-troop friendships. Camporees are exciting, high-energy events that give scouts a chance to represent Troop 544 with pride.
Annual Multi-troop event Skill competitions Theodore Roosevelt Council
Year-Round
Community Service
Scouting is built on a commitment to others. Troop 544 participates in community service projects throughout the year — from local cleanups to scout-organized initiatives. Every Eagle Scout candidate completes a major community service project that leaves a lasting positive impact on New Hyde Park. Service hours are an integral part of rank advancement at multiple levels.
Year-round Community impact Eagle Scout projects
