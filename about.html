Our Story

About Troop 544

67 years of adventure, brotherhood, and service in New Hyde Park, New York.

Our Mission

Scouting Values That Last a Lifetime

Troop 544 follows the mission of the Boy Scouts of America: to prepare young people to make ethical and moral choices over their lifetimes by instilling in them the values of the Scout Oath and Law.

"On my honor I will do my best to do my duty to God and my country and to obey the Scout Law; to help other people at all times; to keep myself physically strong, mentally awake, and morally straight."

— The Scout Oath

Our Journey

67 Years of History

1958

Troop 544 is Founded

Troop 544 was chartered in New Hyde Park, New York, establishing a local presence for Boy Scouts in the community. The troop has been serving scouts and families ever since, building a legacy of character and outdoor adventure.

1960s–1970s

Growth and Community Roots

Through the decades, Troop 544 built strong ties with Manor Oaks School and the New Hyde Park community, developing lasting traditions of weekly meetings, camping trips, and merit badge achievement.

1980s–1990s

Eagle Scouts and Leadership

Troop 544 produced numerous Eagle Scouts during these decades — the highest rank in Scouting — cementing the troop's reputation for producing well-rounded, community-minded young men.

2000s–2010s

Expanding Programs

The troop expanded its programming to include shooting sports, District Camporees, and an annual summer camp experience, giving scouts more opportunities for achievement and growth.

Today

67+ Years Strong

Today Troop 544 continues its proud tradition, meeting every Wednesday evening at Manor Oaks School. We welcome scouts of all experience levels and are always looking for new members to join our family.

Theodore Roosevelt Council

Part of a Larger Brotherhood

Troop 544 is chartered under the Boy Scouts of America's Theodore Roosevelt Council, which serves Nassau County and surrounding areas of New York.

This connection gives our scouts access to council-wide events, camps, and resources that go beyond what any single troop can offer on its own.
