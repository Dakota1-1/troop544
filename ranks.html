The Path to Eagle

Seven Ranks. One Journey.

The BSA rank advancement program gives scouts a structured path to grow — each rank builds on the last, adding new knowledge, skills, and leadership responsibilities. The journey culminates in Eagle Scout, the highest rank in Scouting.

1
Scout
The entry rank. Learn the Scout Oath, Law, and basic scouting knowledge. Your adventure begins here.
2
Tenderfoot
Learn first aid basics, Scout sign & salute, and participate in your first campout as a full troop member.
3
Second Class
Master outdoor skills: cooking, navigation with map and compass, and first aid for injuries and emergencies.
4
First Class
Advanced outdoor skills, swimming, first aid, and leadership within your patrol. The foundation of the full scouting skill set.
5
Star Scout
Earn 6 merit badges (4 Eagle-required), demonstrate leadership in the troop, and complete community service hours.
6
Life Scout
Earn 11 merit badges (7 Eagle-required), hold a troop leadership position, and expand your service to the community.
7
Eagle Scout
The highest rank in Scouting. Earn 21 merit badges (13 required), demonstrate exceptional leadership, and complete a major community service project that leaves a lasting positive impact. Less than 4% of all scouts reach Eagle — it is a distinction that lasts a lifetime.

Merit Badges

Merit badges let scouts dive deep into subjects they're passionate about — from rock climbing to robotics, cooking to chess, citizenship to cinematography.

Outdoor Skills

Camping, Hiking, Cooking, Pioneering, Wilderness Survival, Orienteering, and more — the core of the scouting outdoor experience.

Citizenship

Citizenship in the Community, Nation, and World — three required Eagle badges that build civic knowledge and responsibility.

STEM & Technology

Robotics, Programming, Aviation, Chemistry, Engineering, and Space Exploration — badges for the curious and technically-minded.

Health & Safety

First Aid, Emergency Preparedness, Lifesaving, and Swimming — required Eagle badges that teach scouts to protect themselves and others.

Arts & Communication

Photography, Art, Music, Public Speaking, and Journalism — for scouts who want to express themselves and connect with others.

Personal Development

Personal Management, Family Life, Personal Fitness — Eagle-required badges that build the habits and skills of a responsible adult.

Eagle Scout Requirements

To earn Eagle Scout, a scout must: earn 21 merit badges (including 13 required ones), demonstrate active leadership in the troop for 6 months while Life Scout, earn total of 6 hours of community service, and complete an Eagle Scout service project approved by the troop and council.

Digital Advancement Tracking

Track Progress on Scoutbook

Troop 544 uses Scoutbook — the official BSA digital advancement tracking platform — to record merit badge completions, rank requirements, service hours, and camping nights. Parents and scouts can log in anytime to check progress toward the next rank.
